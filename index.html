About me

Yang Yaning, Ph.D., is currently unemployed.

In 2023, he received a doctorate degree in computer science and technology from Hunan University. His main research directions include high-performance computing, genome sequence comparison, and genome structure variation detection. In recent years, he has published more than 10 papers in important domestic and foreign journals and conferences such as Briefings in Bioinformatics, IEEE/ACM Transactions on Computational Biology and Bioinformatics, Bioinformatics, and Nature Machine Intelligence. Participated in many projects such as the National Key R&D Program, the General Project of the National Natural Science Foundation of China, and the Natural Science Foundation of Hunan Province. Served as a reviewer for BIBM, HPCC, ISBRA, ICIC, IJPRAI, Bioinformatics and other conferences and journals.

What i'm doing

  • High Performance Computing

    Large-scale parallel computing framework and parallel program optimization, including homogeneous multi-core (general computing) and heterogeneous many-core (such as NVIDIA GPU, MT-3000 CPU).

  • Computational Genomics

    Sequence alignment, structural variation detection, variation pathogenicity prediction, etc. based on High-throughput sequencing, Single-molecule real-time (Pacific Biosciences, Oxford Nanopore).

  • Transcriptomics

    Clustering and classification of gene expression profiles, single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) data analysis.

  • Multi-omics Integration

    Combined with machine learning to conduct correlation analysis on multiple levels of omics data such as genome, transcriptome, proteome, and metabolome of specific diseases or tissues.
